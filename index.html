OVERVIEW

I am a statistician, currently serving in the Republic of Korea Navy as a research specialist within the Naval Future Innovation Research Group, a direct-report unit to Naval Headquarters. My work supports the Academic Liaison Officer in research planning and evaluation with additional involvement in operations research using Bayesian search and detection models. My academic background is in Bayesian statistics with research interests spanning Bayesian nonparametric modeling, distributed lag models for mixture exposures, and applications in public health and air pollution.

PROJECTS

R package: dlmtree

  • Modeled time-varying exposure-response in mixtures using treed DLMs with Bayesian inference for lagged, nonparametric effects.
  • Incorporated mixture exposures, heterogeneity, non-linear exposure-time-response effects.
  • Developed an R package for efficient implementation of treed DLMs, enabling reproducible analysis, Bayesian diagnostics, and visualization.
Bayesian Inference Hierarchical Modeling Variable Selection MCMC Rcpp Git Package Development HPC Slurm ggplot2 Shiny R Markdown

Urban Natural Gas Leaks and Environmental Injustice

  • Developed spatial Bayesian models to estimate leak densities and environmental disparities across 13 U.S. metro areas.
  • Integrated methane sensor data, GIS boundaries, and standardized census predictors to perform spatial analyses.
  • Produced statistically grounded, policy-relevant insights on how race, income, and housing age shape urban infrastructure risks.
